Hello!

I am Juan Antonio, a first-year Robotics Ph.D. student at the Georgia Institute of Technology. In June 2021, I obtained my B.S. in mechatronics engineering from Tecnologico de Monterrey in Mexico. My research interests involve collaborative robots, human-robot-interaction, and mobile robotics. In addition, I am interested in applications such as education, agriculture, and marine exploration.

I am currently a graduate teaching assistant at Georgia Tech. Previously, I was a research technician at the Institute for Scientific and Technological Research of San Luis Potosi (IPICYT), working on crowdsensing and air quality monitoring systems. In 2019, I interned at Harvard Medical School's Assitant Professor Yu Shrike Zhang's research group working on 3D Digital Light Processing printers, biofabrication, and tissue engineering.

In my undergrad, I had the pleasure to be part of the Robotics Club, the captain of the League of Legends Esports representative team, and the vice-president of the Japanese Cultural Club. At leisure, I enjoy reading, cooking, and studying Japanese.
