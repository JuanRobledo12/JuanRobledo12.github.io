3D Printing

3D Digital Light Processing Printer

I worked on this project during my internship at Harvard Medical School's Prof. Yu Shrike Zhang's laboratory. The objective was to create a portable, low-cost device that could handle various materials such as commercial resins and hydrogel-based inks. The printer can be used in several applications related to tissue engineering and education.

This device uses digital light processing (DLP) technology based on a projected light source to cure an entire layer at once. Therefore the printing speed is increased compared to other light-based technologies such as stereolithography (SLA). I participated in the design and implementation of the device's hardware. In addition, I drew and rendered images of the printer in SolidWorks. Please, check our work in more detail in the research article published in Advance Materials.

The team in charge of developing this device has also participated in a progress report published in Advanced Healthcare Materials. If you are interested, please check it out here.
