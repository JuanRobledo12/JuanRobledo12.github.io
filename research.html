Research

I am interested in agricultural robotics, especially robot harvesting and crop monitoring through perception techniques. Intelligent robot systems can help to enhance crop yield and reduce food loss at the beginning of the supply chain. In addition, manual harvesting is a repetitive process with prolonged sun exposure that can lead to injuries. Therefore, robots can collaborate with farmers to reduce their exposure to dangerous situations.

On the other hand, I am interested in collaborative robots and human-robot interaction with applications in education. Robots can be a potent tool in an academic setting since they can provide students personalized assistance, tutoring, and assignment grading. Finally, robots can reduce the professors' workload in a course allowing them to focus on giving the students a better learning experience.

Previously, I've researched crowdsensing techniques, air quality monitoring systems, biofabrication, 3D printing, and tissue engineering. If you are interested in knowing more about my research, please visit my ResearchGate profile.
